Admissions overview

The goal is to reduce parent uncertainty before enrollment.

Parents need to know what happens after they inquire. This admissions page turns the process into clear steps: inquiry, level check, trial class, teacher match, schedule, and monthly plan.

Best first action

Book a free trial or level check before choosing a monthly package.

What parents share

Child age, current level, program interest, class format, and schedule preference.

What parents receive

A recommended learning path, schedule option, and next practice goals.

Enrollment steps

From inquiry to confident enrollment.

1. Inquiry

Parent shares the child's age, goals, and preferred program.

2. Level check

The child's current level and learning needs are understood.

3. Trial class

The parent sees teaching style and child comfort before committing.

4. Enrollment

The family chooses the schedule, format, and monthly plan.

Pricing guidance

Publish pricing only when packages are confirmed.

For launch, keep pricing transparent during inquiry rather than publishing unconfirmed numbers. Once capacity and teacher availability are clear, add monthly packages for one-to-one, group, and online classes.

Parent readiness

What parents should know before booking a trial.

A clear admissions page removes friction. Parents should understand what information is needed, what the child will experience, and what happens after the trial.

Before trial

Share the child profile

Age, current level, confidence, previous classes, preferred language, and parent goals help match the child correctly.

During trial

Observe comfort and fit

The trial should show how the teacher interacts, how the child responds, and whether the class format feels suitable.

After trial

Choose a realistic plan

The enrollment recommendation should include subject path, class format, schedule, and suggested practice routine.

Trial flow

A parent-friendly trial should feel simple.

1. Inquiry

  • Parent sends details
  • Program interest is noted
  • Schedule preference is captured

2. Assessment

  • Teacher checks level
  • Child comfort is observed
  • Learning needs are noted

3. Recommendation

  • Best program path
  • One-to-one or group
  • Suggested weekly frequency

4. Follow-up

  • Parent questions answered
  • Package confirmed
  • Start date selected

Trust before payment

What should be confirmed before enrollment.

Teacher details

Share verified teacher name, subject strength, teaching style, and availability before the family commits.

Class expectations

Confirm class length, frequency, attendance rules, parent communication, and how missed classes are handled.

Progress reporting

Explain how parents will receive lesson notes, practice targets, and milestone updates.

Do not publish fixed pricing, certifications, or teacher claims until the details are verified. This protects trust and avoids overpromising.

Admissions FAQs

Questions parents ask before enrolling.

Is the trial class free?

The site is structured around a free trial offer. Confirm final trial policy before wide promotion.

Can parents choose one-to-one or group classes?

Yes, the site supports both inquiry paths. Final availability depends on schedule and teacher capacity.

When should we choose a package?

After the level check or trial class, so the package matches the child's learning needs.

What information should I send before the trial?

Send the child's age, current level, program interest, preferred class type, schedule preference, and any previous learning experience.

Will pricing be shared before enrollment?

Yes. Pricing should be shared clearly during the inquiry or follow-up stage once the class format and schedule are confirmed.

Can we change programs later?

Yes. A child may begin with Quran reading and later add Arabic or Islamic studies if the schedule remains manageable.
