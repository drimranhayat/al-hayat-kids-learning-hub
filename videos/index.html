Video Coverage

Safe video learning categories

This page uses lesson preview cards now. Later, you can connect your own verified YouTube videos or recorded lessons without changing the structure.

Step 1

Choose the right video level

Good video learning should be short, purposeful and followed by speaking, drawing, practice or a parent question.

Ages 3–4

Watch, Point & Repeat

Best for songs, letters, colors, sounds and picture talk.

  • Short videos
  • Repeat words
  • Point to objects
  • Move and sing
Ages 5–6

Listen, Say & Match

Best for phonics, numbers, shapes, manners and simple vocabulary.

  • Say key words
  • Match picture
  • Answer one question
  • Draw one idea
Ages 7–8

Watch, Answer & Practice

Best for reading, math facts, science observation and Islamic values.

  • Watch lesson
  • Answer question
  • Complete activity
  • Explain learning
Ages 9–10

Think, Explain & Apply

Best for deeper explanations, problem solving and short reflections.

  • Take notes
  • Explain concept
  • Apply to life
  • Correct mistake
Ages 11–12

Independent Video Learning

Best for structured revision, project ideas and self-checking.

  • Watch with goal
  • Write summary
  • Ask question
  • Do follow-up task
Parent Tip

Watch Less, Learn More

A short video plus a task is better than long passive screen time.

  • Set purpose
  • Watch together
  • Ask one question
  • Do one activity

Parent & Teacher Zone

Safe video learning guidance

Videos should support learning, not replace reading, writing, play, family talk or outdoor movement.

Before Watching

Set purpose and time.

  • Choose one suitable lesson
  • Tell the child what to notice
  • Keep volume comfortable
  • Avoid random autoplay

During Watching

Make the child active.

  • Pause and ask questions
  • Repeat key words
  • Point to examples
  • Keep video short

After Watching

Turn video into practice.

  • Ask what was learned
  • Do one worksheet or activity
  • Take an eye break
  • Connect lesson to real life

🌙 20-Minute Safe Video Learning Routine

Use this simple routine so screen time becomes active learning, not passive watching.

  • 2 minutes: Choose one lesson and set a clear purpose.
  • 8 minutes: Watch the lesson preview or verified video with attention.
  • 3 minutes: Child says three things they learned.
  • 5 minutes: Complete a drawing, worksheet or quick activity.
  • 2 minutes: Take an eye break and move away from the screen.
