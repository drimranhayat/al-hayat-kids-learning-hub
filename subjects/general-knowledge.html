Parent & Teacher Zone

General Knowledge curriculum method and map

The child area stays focused on selected age and lesson. Parent guidance is placed here, away from the main learning space.

Open: How young children should learn General Knowledge

Ages 3–7 should learn General Knowledge through observation, real-life examples, pictures, conversation, stories, role play, field awareness, drawing, quizzes, and kind behaviour.

👀

Observe

Look carefully at people, places, animals, weather, maps, and objects.

💬

Ask

Use simple questions: What is it? Where is it? Who helps us? Why is it important?

🎭

Role Play

Act as a doctor, teacher, shopkeeper, pilot, farmer, explorer, or scientist.

✍️

Draw & Tell

Children remember better when they draw, point, name, and explain.

Open: General Knowledge learning worlds map

The curriculum gradually covers self, family, home, school, helpers, animals, plants, food, weather, transport, safety, manners, environment, maps, countries, flags, space, inventions, public places, and responsible citizenship.

  • Use one age level for several weeks before moving forward.
  • Do one lesson at a time; do not overload the child.
  • Audio is short: fact, question, mission, or answer only.
  • Children should observe real life before memorizing facts.
  • Mix home items: books, fruits, toys, clock, calendar, plants, maps, water, bags, and pictures.
