World Lab

Learn directions and continents

Children can begin global awareness with simple map skills: directions, continents, oceans, countries, and respectful curiosity about people.

🧭 Compass Directions

A compass helps us understand directions. Start with four main directions: north, south, east, and west.

N S E W

Practice: Ask your child: Which direction is shown at the top of the compass?

🌍 Continents

Many schools teach seven continents. Children can learn them through songs, maps, puzzles, and repeated visual practice.

Learning Pathway

A strong GK foundation in four steps

Children remember facts better when they connect them with places, pictures, stories, and questions.

1

Learn

Start with one simple fact, word, place, country, or invention.

2

Locate

Find it on a map, picture, object, timeline, or daily-life example.

3

Question

Ask who, what, where, when, why, and how questions.

4

Share

Explain the fact in your own words and connect it with life.

For Parents & Teachers

How to build global awareness kindly

General knowledge should make children curious, respectful, and thoughtful. It should not become pressure to memorize random facts without understanding.

  • Use maps, globes, pictures, stories, and real objects whenever possible.
  • Teach children to respect all people, languages, cultures, and countries.
  • Ask children to explain facts in their own words instead of only repeating them.
  • Connect GK with Islamic values: gratitude, respect, care for creation, and love of beneficial knowledge.
  • Keep practice short, joyful, and conversation-based.
