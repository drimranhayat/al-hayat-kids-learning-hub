Today’s Math Practice

One small problem, one clear strategy

Children should learn how to think, not only how to answer. Today’s practice uses dots, number facts, and a short quiz.

➕ Addition of the Day

4 + 3 = 7

Think: Start with 4, then count 3 more: 5, 6, 7.

➖ Subtraction of the Day

9 − 2 = 7

Think: Start with 9, take away 2, and count what remains.

📘 Word Problem

Amina has 5 pencils. Her mother gives her 2 more pencils. How many pencils does Amina have now?

For Parents & Teachers

How to help children love math

Math anxiety often begins when children feel rushed or embarrassed. Keep math slow, visual, encouraging, and connected to daily life.

  • Use real objects like spoons, pencils, blocks, fruits, and coins.
  • Ask children to explain their thinking instead of only giving an answer.
  • Use short practice sessions: 5 to 10 minutes can be very effective.
  • Praise effort, strategy, neat work, and improvement.
  • Connect math with daily routines: shopping, time, sharing, cooking, and games.
