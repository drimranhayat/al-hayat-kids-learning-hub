For Parents & Teachers

How to help children love Arabic

Arabic learning should feel warm, slow, playful, and successful. Do not rush children. A few minutes of happy daily practice is better than a long stressful lesson.

  • Practice one letter at a time instead of many letters together.
  • Use large writing, clear cards, colorful worksheets, and repeated sounds.
  • Connect Arabic with familiar Islamic words such as Allah, Bismillah, Salam, and Dua.
  • Praise effort, not only correct answers.
  • Keep pronunciation gentle; difficult throat sounds can improve gradually with listening and guidance.
