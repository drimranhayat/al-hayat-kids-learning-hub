Open: How young children should learn Arabic

Ages 3–7 should learn Arabic through seeing letters, hearing sounds, saying short words, tracing, matching, playing, and repeating. Reading comes gradually after sound and shape recognition.

Open: Arabic Learning Worlds curriculum map

The full curriculum is built around letter recognition, sounds, vocabulary, harakat, joining, reading, writing, and simple Arabic sentences.

  • Use one level for several weeks before moving forward.
  • Do one lesson at a time; do not overload the child.
  • Audio is short: letter, sound, word, story, quiz, or mission only.
  • Browser Arabic voice is helpful, but real Arabic MP3 is better for exact sound quality.
  • Young children learn Arabic best through playful repetition and tracing.
