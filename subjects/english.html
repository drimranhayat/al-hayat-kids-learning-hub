Reading Practice

The Helpful Friend

A short reading passage about kindness, friendship, and helping others.

⏱️ 4 minutes 👧 Ages 6–10 🌱 Value: Kindness
Visit Reading Library

Sara saw her friend Amina sitting quietly in the playground. Amina had lost her pencil box and looked worried. Sara smiled and said, “Do not worry. I will help you find it.”

They looked near the bench, under the tree, and beside the classroom door. At last, Sara found the pencil box on a chair. Amina thanked her happily.

Sara said, “Friends help each other.” Amina smiled and promised to help others too.

Comprehension Questions

  • What did Amina lose?
  • How did Sara help her friend?
  • What value do we learn from this story?

For Parents & Teachers

How to help children love English

English learning should feel useful, kind, and confidence-building. Small daily reading, speaking, and writing routines are better than long stressful lessons.

  • Read one short passage daily and ask two simple questions.
  • Teach words with examples, not only meanings.
  • Let children speak without fear of mistakes; correct gently.
  • Use stories, pictures, games, and real-life sentences.
  • Praise effort, clear pronunciation, and complete sentences.
