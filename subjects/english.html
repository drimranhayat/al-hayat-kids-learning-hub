Meaning: ready to do the right thing even when it feels difficult.

🧩 Grammar Bite

Adjective: A word that describes a noun.

The kind girl helped her friend.

In this sentence, kind describes the girl.

Reading Practice

Read, listen, and understand

This short passage builds fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Children can listen first, then read aloud.

📖 Passage: The Little Garden

Sara had a small garden near her home. Every morning, she gave water to the plants. She watched the tiny leaves grow bigger day by day.

One afternoon, Sara saw a dry plant. She did not ignore it. She added water and moved it to a place with more sunlight.

After a few days, the plant looked fresh again. Sara smiled and said, “Small care can make a big difference.”

✅ Comprehension Questions

1. What did Sara have near her home?

2. What happened to one plant?

3. How did Sara help the plant?

4. What lesson can we learn from the story?

Vocabulary: garden, tiny, ignore, sunlight, fresh, difference.
Speaking Practice

Speak with confidence

Speaking improves when children hear a model, repeat the sentence, and answer in their own words.

🗣️ Speaking Prompt

Talk for 30 seconds:

My favourite book is...

Try to include: title, character, reason, and one lesson.

🎯 Good Speaking Checklist

Speak slowly. Use clear words. Look confident. Give one reason. Finish with a complete sentence.

For Parents & Teachers

How to help children love English

English learning should feel safe, repeated, meaningful, and encouraging. Listening support helps children hear rhythm and pronunciation before they speak.

  • Use the Listen button first, then ask the child to repeat aloud.
  • Do not correct every small mistake immediately; protect confidence.
  • Ask children to use new words in their own sentences.
  • Read short passages repeatedly because repetition builds fluency.
  • Keep practice short: 10 focused minutes every day is powerful.
