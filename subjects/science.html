Learning Method

A science routine children can follow

Good science learning starts with curiosity. Children should observe, ask questions, make simple predictions, test safely, and explain what they noticed.

👀

Observe

Look closely at objects, animals, plants, weather, light, sound, and movement.

❓

Ask

Ask simple questions such as: What is happening? Why? How can we know?

🧪

Test

Try safe activities with adult help, using everyday materials and careful steps.

💬

Explain

Use simple words, drawings, and examples to explain what was discovered.

💧 Water Cycle Steps

The Sun warms water in seas, rivers, lakes, and puddles. Some water changes into water vapour and rises into the air.

🧪 Safe Home Experiment: Sink or Float

This simple activity helps children observe, predict, test, and explain. Adult supervision is recommended.

Materials: bowl of water, spoon, small plastic cap, coin, leaf, pencil.
Question: Which objects will sink? Which will float?
Task: Predict first. Test gently. Then discuss what happened.
Safety: Keep water shallow, avoid sharp objects, and clean the area after the activity.

For Parents & Teachers

How to help children love science

Science should feel like safe discovery, not memorization only. Let children ask questions, touch safe materials, draw observations, and explain in their own words.

  • Use real examples from home: water, light, plants, food, weather, shadows, and sounds.
  • Ask “What do you notice?” before giving the answer.
  • Let children predict first, then test safely with adult supervision.
  • Encourage drawing and labeling because children often understand before they can write long answers.
  • Connect science with care for Allah’s creation: kindness to animals, plants, water, and the environment.
