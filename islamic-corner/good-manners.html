Why good manners matter

Children should not only memorize Islamic facts. They should learn to speak gently, respect others, care for belongings, keep promises and practise kindness every day.

Teaching rule: Keep lessons simple. Read one manner, discuss one example, practise one action, then praise the child sincerely.

Daily Manners Missions

👋

Salam Mission

Say salam clearly to family members and reply with respect.

💧

Cleanliness Mission

Wash hands, keep desk tidy, and put one item back in its place.

❤️

Kind Words Mission

Use “please”, “thank you”, “sorry”, and “may Allah bless you”.

🤲

Gratitude Mission

Name three blessings and thank Allah for them.

Parent & Teacher Guide

Good manners grow through modelling. Adults should show the behaviour first, then ask children to practise gently. Avoid harsh correction in public. Use short reminders, stories, duas and daily praise.
