Journey Map

Learn prophets in a clear order

This map helps children see the broad Qur’anic learning journey while avoiding unsupported dates, legends, or invented details.

Tawḥīd • Truth • Patience • Justice • Guidance
🌍

Earliest Prophets

Adam and Idris: creation, truthfulness, repentance, and early guidance.

🚢

Early Nations

Nuh, Hud, Salih, Shuayb: warning, patience, fairness, and obedience.

🕋

Family of Ibrahim

Ibrahim, Ismail, Ishaq, Yaqub, Yusuf: Tawḥīd, family, sacrifice, and forgiveness.

🌊

Bani Israel

Musa and Harun: courage, teamwork, patience, and standing against oppression.

🌱

Later Prophets

Ayyub, Yunus, Zakariya, Yahya, Isa and others: dua, purity, patience, and hope.

🌙

Final Messenger

Muhammad ﷺ: mercy, final guidance, noble character, and Sunnah.

Belief & Character Outcomes

What children should learn

Stories of prophets are not entertainment only. They teach belief, manners, worship, courage, justice, and source-care.

☝️

Tawḥīd

Every prophet called people to worship Allah alone.

⛰️

Patience

Prophets stayed firm when people mocked, rejected, or harmed them.

⚖️

Justice

Prophets taught fair worship, fair speech, fair trade, and fair community life.

🔍

Source Care

We learn what is revealed and avoid fake stories or unsupported details.

Authenticity Note

Careful, Qur’an-based, and child-friendly

The page uses broad Qur’anic references and avoids fictional details. Teachers can expand with reliable tafsir, authentic hadith, and trusted Seerah resources.

Key Qur’an reference lists

  • Qur’an 6:83–86 — many guided prophets named
  • Qur’an 4:163–164 — revelation to many prophets
  • Qur’an 4:164 — some messengers narrated, others not
  • Qur’an 40:78 — messengers before, some not related in detail

No fabrication rule

Do not add exact ages, invented dialogues, dramatic scenes, miracles, or details not supported by Qur’an, authentic hadith, or reliable scholarship.

Teaching approach

For children, keep the focus on faith and values: Tawḥīd, truthfulness, patience, dua, gratitude, justice, mercy, modesty, and respect for all prophets.
