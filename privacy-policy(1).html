Privacy Policy for Families

We care about children’s safety, parent trust, and respectful learning. This page explains what information may be used on Al-Hayat Kids Learning Hub, how progress features work, how parents can contact us, and how families can use the website safely.

🌿 1. Overview

Al-Hayat Kids Learning Hub is an educational website created for children, parents, and teachers. It provides worksheets, stories, Islamic learning pages, quizzes, activities, and learning resources.

This policy explains our general approach to privacy. It is written in clear language for families and schools. It is not a substitute for legal advice, and it may be updated as the website grows.

Last updated: 15 June 2026.

🧒 2. Children’s privacy

Children’s privacy is especially important. Children should use this website with the guidance of a parent, guardian, or teacher. Children should not submit personal information such as full name, home address, phone number, school address, private photos, or passwords through this website.

  • Parents should supervise online learning, especially when children open external links.
  • Children should use first names or nicknames only when a learning activity asks for a name line on a printable worksheet.
  • Any online class inquiry should be made by a parent or guardian, not by a child alone.

📌 3. Information we may receive

The main learning pages can be viewed without creating an account. If a parent contacts us voluntarily, we may receive information such as a name, email address, phone or WhatsApp number, child’s grade level, preferred course, and message content.

We use this information only to respond to inquiries, guide parents, improve learning support, or manage online class requests.

Website useMost pages are open for reading and practice.

Class requestGrade and subject details help us guide parents.

💾 4. Local progress storage

Some interactive pages use browser local storage to remember progress, badges, completed worksheets, completed story cards, quiz progress, or learning preferences on the same device.

  • This progress is generally stored inside the user’s own browser.
  • It helps children continue learning without needing an account.
  • Clearing browser data may remove this saved progress.
  • Progress saved on one device may not appear on another device.
Current interactive features are designed for simple learning progress, not for collecting sensitive child profiles.

📩 5. Contact forms, email, and WhatsApp

If the website includes a contact form, email link, WhatsApp link, or online class inquiry link, the parent or guardian may choose to send information to us. We may use that information to respond to the request.

  • Please do not send unnecessary personal details about a child.
  • Please do not send passwords, payment card details, or private identity documents through ordinary contact messages.
  • Parents may ask us to delete or correct inquiry information they previously sent.

🔗 6. Third-party links and embedded resources

The website may link to educational resources, Qur’an or hadith websites, videos, printable materials, GitHub Pages, or other learning platforms. These external websites are not controlled by Al-Hayat Kids Learning Hub.

  • External websites may use their own cookies, analytics, ads, or privacy policies.
  • Parents should review external websites before children use them independently.
  • We aim to recommend helpful and respectful resources, but external content may change over time.
Parent supervision is strongly recommended whenever children leave this website through an external link.

🛡️ 7. Safety and security

We aim to keep the website safe, respectful, and child-friendly. However, no online system can be promised to be perfectly secure. Families should use safe browsing habits and avoid sharing private information online.

  • Use trusted devices and updated browsers.
  • Do not share passwords with children or strangers.
  • Parents should guide children about safe online behavior.
  • Report any broken, unsafe, or inappropriate link through the contact page.

👨‍👩‍👧 8. Parent and guardian choices

Parents and guardians may contact us to ask questions about privacy, online class inquiries, or information they sent voluntarily.

  • You may ask what information you previously sent through contact or inquiry messages.
  • You may request correction or deletion of inquiry information where reasonably possible.
  • You may clear local progress from your own browser settings.
  • You may choose not to use interactive progress features.

📝 9. Updates to this policy

This privacy policy may be updated when new pages, class inquiry features, forms, analytics, audio resources, or other website features are added. The updated date will be changed when major updates are made.

📬 10. Contact us

For privacy questions, parent concerns, broken links, or online class inquiries, please use the contact page.

Need help or correction?

Parents and teachers can contact us for support, feedback, or privacy-related questions.
