🧭 Guided Reading Path

Five-step story method for parents and teachers

Use this method to turn every story into reading practice, moral understanding and daily action.

Picture talk

Look at the title and emoji. Ask: “What do you think this story will teach?”

Read calmly

Read the short story summary. Let the child repeat important words.

Ask one question

Use the reflection question. Let the child answer in their own language first if needed.

Choose a mission

Practise one value at home: salam, helping, truthfulness, patience or gratitude.

Retell

Ask the child to retell the story in three sentences. Praise effort and clarity.

🏠 Home & Classroom Use

Ready-made Islamic story routines

These routines make the page useful for parents, tutors, weekend schools and classroom teachers.

🌙

Bedtime Islamic Story

Time: 8–10 minutes

Read one story, ask one question, make one dua, and end with a peaceful reminder.

🏫

Class Warm-up

Time: 5–7 minutes

Read the summary, ask pupils to identify the value, and connect it to classroom manners.

📝

Writing Extension

Time: 12–15 minutes

Children write: “The lesson I learned is…” and “Today I will practise it by…”.

⭐ Parent Promise

Make Islamic stories gentle, joyful and practical

Children remember stories when adults make them warm, safe and connected to real life.

  • Do not use stories only to scold children.
  • Praise one good quality after each story.
  • Let children ask questions freely and kindly.
  • Connect each story to one daily habit.
  • Repeat favourite stories; repetition builds love and understanding.
  • Use simple language for younger children and deeper reflection for older children.
