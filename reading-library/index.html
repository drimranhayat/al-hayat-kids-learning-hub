Step 1

Choose the child’s reading level

Children should not be rushed. Start with picture reading and listening, then move toward words, sentences, short stories, comprehension, and independent reading.

Ages 3–4

Picture & Sound Reading

Best for early learners who need pictures, sounds, repetition, and very short story sentences.

  • Listen and point
  • Repeat words
  • Identify characters
  • Say one-word answers
Ages 5–6

Word & Sentence Reading

Best for children learning phonics, sight words, simple sentences, and short story order.

  • Read simple words
  • Match picture to sentence
  • Answer who/what questions
  • Retell in 2–3 lines
Ages 7–8

Short Story Reading

Best for children who can read paragraphs and begin understanding character, setting, and moral.

  • Read short stories
  • Find the main idea
  • Learn new vocabulary
  • Answer comprehension questions
Ages 9–10

Fluency & Meaning

Best for children developing expression, paragraph reading, inference, and summary skills.

  • Read with expression
  • Explain character actions
  • Summarize the story
  • Connect story to real life
Ages 11–12

Independent Reading

Best for children ready for deeper comprehension, reflection, vocabulary, and writing responses.

  • Identify theme
  • Explain moral lesson
  • Write a short review
  • Compare two stories
Parent Tip

Use One Level at a Time

Do not overload the child. One story, one value, one question, and one speaking mission are enough for a daily habit.

  • 10–15 minutes daily
  • Praise effort
  • Read aloud first
  • Let the child repeat

Step 4

Reading missions

A child should not only read silently. The best reading habit includes listening, speaking, understanding, and applying the lesson in real life.

🔊 Listen

Listen to the story once before reading. Notice the title, characters, and important words.

📖 Read

Read slowly. Stop at full stops. Try to understand the meaning, not only the words.

🎙️ Speak

Tell the story in your own words. Say who was in the story and what happened.

✍️ Write

Write one new word, one sentence, and one moral lesson from the story.

Parent & Teacher Zone

How to use this reading hub

The child area should remain joyful and simple. Parents and teachers can use this section to guide reading without pressure.

For Ages 3–5

Focus on listening, picture talk, repeating words, and naming characters.

  • Ask: Who is this?
  • Ask: What can you see?
  • Let the child repeat one sentence.

For Ages 6–8

Focus on reading short sentences, retelling the story, and learning new words.

  • Ask: What happened first?
  • Ask: What was the problem?
  • Ask: What did we learn?

For Ages 9–12

Focus on summary, moral reflection, character actions, and personal response.

  • Ask: Why did the character do this?
  • Ask: What is the main idea?
  • Ask: How can we apply this lesson?

🌙 15-Minute Daily Reading Routine

Use this simple routine at home, especially before homework or before bedtime.

  • 2 minutes: Look at the title and predict the story.
  • 4 minutes: Listen to the story or parent reading.
  • 4 minutes: Child reads selected lines aloud.
  • 3 minutes: Discuss new words and the moral.
  • 2 minutes: Child says one sentence: “Today I learned...”
