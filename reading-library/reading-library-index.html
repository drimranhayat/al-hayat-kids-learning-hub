The Honest Boy

A short original moral story for reading practice. It teaches honesty, responsibility, and courage to tell the truth.

📖

The Honest Boy

A gentle story about truthfulness.

⏱️ 5 minutes 👧 Ages 6–10 🌱 Value: Honesty 📚 Level: Easy
Honest Telling the truth.
Promise Words we should keep.
Courage Doing the right thing even when it is hard.

One morning, a young boy named Hamza found a small wallet near the school gate. The wallet had some money and a little card inside it.

Hamza looked around. Many children were walking quickly to their classrooms. No one seemed to notice the wallet on the ground.

For a moment, Hamza felt confused. He wanted to buy a new pencil box, and the money looked useful. But then he remembered what his mother always said: “A truthful person is trusted by people.”

Hamza picked up the wallet and went straight to his teacher. “I found this near the gate,” he said. “I think someone has lost it.”

The teacher smiled kindly and checked the card. Soon, a worried student came to the office. It was his wallet. He was very happy to get it back.

“Thank you, Hamza,” the student said. “You helped me today.”

Hamza felt peaceful. He did not get a new pencil box that day, but he gained something better: the happiness of doing the right thing.

Think and Talk

  • What did Hamza find near the school gate?
  • Why was it difficult for him to decide what to do?
  • What value did Hamza show?
  • What would you do if you found something that belonged to someone else?
Daily Reading Practice

One story, one word, one reflection

Daily reading should be small, warm, and consistent. A short story plus discussion can build vocabulary, fluency, confidence, and values.

🌟 Word of the Day

Honest

Meaning: someone who tells the truth and does not cheat.

Sentence: Hamza was honest when he returned the wallet.

Predicting

Before reading, look at the title and ask: “What do I think this story will be about?”

For Parents & Teachers

How to help children love reading

Children should not feel reading is punishment. A small daily story, a warm voice, and kind encouragement can transform reading confidence.

  • Read aloud first, then invite the child to read a short part.
  • Do not stop after every small mistake; support meaning and confidence.
  • Ask gentle questions about characters, choices, and values.
  • Let children repeat favorite stories because repetition builds fluency.
  • Connect stories with Islamic manners, kindness, honesty, gratitude, and family life.
